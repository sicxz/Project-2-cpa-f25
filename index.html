How to Use AI in Planning

There's no prescribed method. Use AI however makes sense to you:

Conversational Approach

Open a conversation with Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini and let it evolve naturally. Ask follow-up questions. Push back when suggestions don't fit.

Structured Brainstorming

Ask AI to generate possibilities, suggest tools, identify readings, and reality-check your timeline.

Iterative Refinement

Draft your plan, ask AI to critique it, revise based on feedback, and repeat until it feels right.

Multi-Model Comparison

Ask the same planning question to multiple models, compare suggestions, and synthesize the best ideas.

What matters: The quality of your plan, not the method you used to create it.

Document your process: Include notes about how AI helped in your reflection.

Finding Your Focus

Start with genuine curiosity: What AI + Design topic keeps coming up in your thinking? What do you wish you understood better?

Balance ambition with realism:

  • Too broad: "Explore AI and graphic design" → What aspect? What specific question?
  • Too narrow: "Create 5 logos" → Where's the research? What's the intellectual challenge?
  • Just right: "Create a brand identity using AI while researching industry perspectives on AI-assisted design."

The Goldilocks Principle

  • Focused enough to go deep
  • Broad enough to sustain 7 weeks
  • Ambitious enough to learn significantly
  • Realistic enough to complete successfully

Three-Step Planning Process

Step 1: Design Your 7-Week Study

Use AI to map out what you'll explore and create over 6 weeks (Weeks 6-11).

For each week, define:

  • Primary goal and specific activities
  • Tools/techniques you'll use
  • What you'll create/produce
  • Success indicators

Week structure:

  • Week 6: Getting started, tool exploration
  • Week 7: Going deeper, first prototypes
  • Week 8: Refinement & iteration
  • Week 9: Integration & synthesis
  • Week 10: Polishing & expanding
  • Week 11: Finalizing & presenting

Step 2: Build Reading List & Research Paper Plan

Design the research component running alongside your creative work.

Research Question:

  • Specific, explorable, interesting
  • Connected to design practice

Curate 12-18 Sources:

  • ~40% Academic (journal articles, book chapters)
  • ~40% Practitioner (essays, case studies, tool docs)
  • ~20% Media (podcasts, videos, talks)

Development Strategy:

How will you develop the 3,000-5,000 word paper with AI? (Canvas, Artifacts, Gemini, etc.)

Step 3: Plan Documentation & Final Deliverable

Decide how you'll track progress and what you'll create by Week 11.

Documentation Strategy:

  • Visual note-taking approach
  • Milanote organization
  • Reading response format
  • Weekly reflection method

Final Deliverable Options:

  • Portfolio website (most common)
  • Research paper + visual portfolio
  • Custom tool/prototype
  • Creative artifact + documentation

Your Milanote Board Deliverable

Submit a single, well-organized Milanote board containing all six sections:

  1. Design Focus (Brief Recap) - From Project 1
  2. 7-Week Study Plan - Week-by-week breakdown
  3. Research Paper Plan - Question, sources, strategy
  4. Documentation Strategy - How you'll track progress
  5. Final Deliverable Vision - What you'll create
  6. Reflection (200-400 words) - How AI helped, what excites you, anticipated challenges

Assessment (Planning Phase)

  • 40% Study Plan Quality (clarity, feasibility, alignment, depth)
  • 30% Research Plan Quality (strong question, quality sources, development strategy)
  • 15% Documentation & Deliverable Planning
  • 15% Reflection & Presentation Quality
